# Best Laptops for University Students in Kenya

A student laptop has to survive four years of lecture notes, assignments, Zoom classes and the occasional all-nighter — without breaking your budget. Here is how to choose.

## The specs that actually matter

- **RAM: 8GB minimum, 16GB if you can.** This is the single biggest factor in whether a laptop still feels fast in year three.
- **Storage: an SSD, 256GB or more.** Never buy a laptop with a slow mechanical hard drive in 2026 — an SSD makes everything feel instant.
- **A modern processor.** An Intel Core i5 / Ryzen 5 (recent generation) is the value sweet spot. Core i3 / Ryzen 3 is fine for pure browsing and Office.
- **Battery life.** Aim for a real-world 6+ hours so you are not hunting for sockets between lectures.

## Match the laptop to the course

- **Business, law, arts:** a light, long-battery ultrabook is ideal. You mostly need Office, a browser and good battery.
- **Engineering, architecture, design:** you need 16GB RAM and a dedicated GPU for CAD, rendering or heavy software.
- **Computer science:** prioritise RAM and a fast SSD over GPU; you will run VMs and containers.

## New vs refurbished

A [refurbished business laptop](/guides/buying-a-refurbished-laptop-in-kenya) (think ThinkPad, EliteBook, Latitude) often gives you better build quality and more RAM than a new budget consumer laptop at the same price. For students, this is frequently the smartest buy.
