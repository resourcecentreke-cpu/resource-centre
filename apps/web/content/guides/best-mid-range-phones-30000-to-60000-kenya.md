# Best Mid-Range Phones in Kenya (KSh 30,000–60,000)

This is the band where a phone stops feeling like a compromise. You get flagship-grade screens, cameras that hold up in low light, and chips fast enough to last you three or four years.

## What your money buys here

- **A genuinely good main camera** with optical stabilisation, so night shots and video stop looking shaky.
- **A bright AMOLED display**, usually 120Hz — easier to read in Nairobi sunlight and smoother to use.
- **Faster charging** (45W–100W on some models), so a 15-minute top-up gets you through the day.
- **Stronger build** — glass backs, better water resistance, and in-display fingerprint readers.

## The trade-offs to weigh

At the lower end (KSh 30k–40k) you are choosing between a great screen *or* a great camera — rarely both. By KSh 50k–60k you usually get both, plus longer software support.

If you keep phones for years, lean toward brands with longer update commitments (Samsung and Google lead here). If you upgrade often, the Chinese flagships (Xiaomi, OnePlus, Honor) give you more raw hardware for the price.

## Practical advice for Kenya

Check that the model supports the **5G bands** rolling out in Nairobi and major towns if you want to future-proof — though [4G is still perfectly fine](/guides/4g-vs-5g-in-kenya-is-5g-worth-it) for most users. And always [compare seller prices](/c/smartphones): mid-range phones see the biggest price gaps between shops.
