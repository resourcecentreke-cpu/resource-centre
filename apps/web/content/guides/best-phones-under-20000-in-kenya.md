# Best Phones Under KSh 20,000 in Kenya

Twenty thousand shillings is the sweet spot of the Kenyan market — it is where Tecno, Infinix, Xiaomi (Redmi) and Samsung fight hardest, so you get more phone per shilling than at any other price. The trick is knowing which corners are safe to cut.

## What to prioritise

- **A big, healthy battery.** Aim for 5,000mAh or more. In a market with frequent power interruptions, all-day (or two-day) battery beats almost any other feature.
- **At least 6GB RAM and 128GB storage.** This keeps WhatsApp, M-Pesa, browser tabs and a couple of social apps running without constant reloads, and leaves room for photos.
- **A 90Hz or 120Hz screen** if you can find one. Smooth scrolling is the single biggest "feels expensive" upgrade at this price.
- **4G with the right bands.** Every phone here should handle Safaricom and Airtel 4G comfortably.

## What you can safely ignore

Megapixel counts on the box are mostly marketing. A clean 50MP main camera with good software beats a "108MP" sensor that produces mushy photos. Likewise, ignore "AI" branding — it rarely means anything at this price.

## Brands that deliver

Tecno and Infinix dominate this band on value and battery. Xiaomi's Redmi line usually has the cleanest software and best screens. Samsung's entry Galaxy A models cost a little more for the same specs, but hold resale value better and get longer security updates.

## How to buy well

Prices for the exact same model swing by KSh 2,000–4,000 between shops. Always [compare across stores](/c/smartphones) before paying, and confirm the phone is the Kenyan/global variant with a local warranty — not a grey-market import.

> Bottom line: pick the biggest battery and most RAM you can get from a reputable seller, and don't pay for megapixels.
