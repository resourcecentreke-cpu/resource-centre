The under-30,000 shillings bracket is the sweet spot of the Kenyan phone market. It's where brands like **Tecno, Infinix, Samsung, Xiaomi/Redmi and Realme** fight hardest, so you get a lot of phone for the money. This guide explains what actually matters at this price, so you don't overpay for specs you'll never use.

## What to prioritise under KSh 30,000

- **Battery and charging.** A 5,000mAh+ battery is the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade. Look for 18W charging or faster.
- **Display.** An AMOLED screen looks far better than LCD and is increasingly common in this range. 90Hz or 120Hz makes scrolling smoother.
- **RAM and storage.** Aim for 8GB RAM and 128GB+ storage so the phone stays smooth for years and you don't run out of space.
- **Software updates.** Samsung and (increasingly) the Transsion brands now promise more Android updates — worth it for security.

## What you can safely skip

- **Headline camera megapixels.** A well-tuned 50MP sensor beats a poorly-tuned 108MP one. Read real photo samples, don't chase the number.
- **"Gaming" branding.** Useful only if you actually play demanding titles; otherwise it's marketing.

## Brands that deliver in this range

- **Tecno & Infinix** — best value for battery, screen and design; widely available and serviced in Kenya.
- **Samsung A-series** — best for software updates and resale value.
- **Xiaomi Redmi** — strong all-rounders, good displays.
- **Realme** — fast charging and bold designs.

Prices for the same phone vary a lot between Kenyan shops. Before you buy:

1. Compare the live price across stores on the **[KSh 23,000–29,999 phones](/phones/price-23000-29999)** and **[KSh 16,000–22,999](/phones/price-16000-22999)** pages.
2. Check the **price history** on the product page — if it's near its lowest, buy; if it spiked recently, wait.
