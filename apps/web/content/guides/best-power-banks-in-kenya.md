# How to Choose a Power Bank in Kenya

A power bank is essential kit in Kenya, where a long day out or a power interruption can leave you stranded. Here is how to pick the right one.

## Capacity: how many charges you get

Capacity is measured in mAh. A rough guide:

- **10,000mAh** — about 2 full phone charges. Pocketable, good for daily carry.
- **20,000mAh** — about 4 charges. The all-rounder most people should buy.
- **27,000mAh+** — for travel, camping, or charging a laptop and phone together.

Note that real-world charges are lower than the maths suggests — energy is lost as heat, so a 20,000mAh bank delivers closer to 13,000–14,000mAh to your phone. That is normal, not a defect.

## Wattage: how fast it charges

Look at the **output wattage (W)**, not just capacity. A 22.5W or 45W bank with Power Delivery (PD) tops up a modern phone far faster than a cheap 10W unit. For charging a laptop, you need 45W–100W PD.

## Ports and extras

- **USB-C with PD** is now the standard — prefer it.
- A **built-in display** showing the remaining percentage is genuinely useful.
- Pass-through charging (charging the bank and your phone at once) is a handy bonus.

## Buying tips

Stick to known brands (Oraimo, Anker, UGREEN, Baseus, Xiaomi) — cheap no-name banks often overstate capacity and can be unsafe. [Compare power bank prices](/c/powerbanks) before buying.
