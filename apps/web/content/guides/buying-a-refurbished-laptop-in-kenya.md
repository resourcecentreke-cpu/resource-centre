A refurbished business laptop — think Dell Latitude, HP EliteBook, Lenovo ThinkPad or a used MacBook — often gives you a better keyboard, screen and build than a brand-new budget laptop at the same price. The catch is condition. Here's how to buy smart.

## Why refurbished is worth considering

- **Premium build for less.** Ex-corporate ThinkPads and EliteBooks are built to survive years of daily use.
- **Better screens and keyboards** than entry-level new laptops in the same budget.
- **More performance per shilling**, since you're not paying the "new" premium.

## The five things to check before paying

1. **Battery health & charge cycles.** This is the most important number. Ask for the battery health percentage and cycle count — under ~500 cycles and 80%+ health is healthy. (On our refurbished listings we show these where known.)
2. **Screen.** Look for dead pixels, backlight bleed and scratches at full brightness on a white background.
3. **Keyboard & trackpad.** Test every key and click. Worn keycaps are cosmetic; non-responsive keys are not.
4. **Ports & hinges.** Wobble the hinge, plug into every USB/HDMI port, test the webcam and speakers.
5. **Storage type & RAM.** Insist on an **SSD** (not a spinning HDD) and at least **8GB RAM** — 16GB if you can.

## Match the laptop to your use

Not sure which model suits you? Our **[laptop chooser](/laptops/chooser)** recommends options by use-case (student, business, programming, design, gaming) and budget, including refurbished units.

- **Students & everyday:** a clean ThinkPad/Latitude with an SSD is unbeatable value.
- **Programming/business:** prioritise 16GB RAM and a comfortable keyboard.
- **Design/creative:** look for a higher-resolution or colour-accurate panel (e.g. XPS, ZenBook OLED, MacBook Pro).

## Warranty & where to buy

Buy from a seller that offers at least a short warranty and a return window — even 7–14 days lets you catch hidden faults. Compare current prices on the **[laptops listing](/c/laptops)** and check each unit's price history before committing.

> A great refurbished laptop is about condition, not just the model name. Spend ten minutes on the checklist above and you'll get years of use.
