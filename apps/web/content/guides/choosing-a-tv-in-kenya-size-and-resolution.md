# How to Choose a TV in Kenya

A TV is a multi-year purchase, so it pays to get the size and panel right. Here is how to choose without overpaying for features you won't use.

## Get the size right for your room

Bigger is usually better — but only to a point. As a rough guide for viewing distance:

- **32"** — bedrooms and small spaces.
- **43"** — the most popular living-room size in Kenya; great all-rounder.
- **50"–55"** — for a proper living-room setup where you sit 2–3 metres away.
- **65"+** — large lounges and home-cinema setups.

## Resolution: is 4K worth it?

- **Full HD (1080p)** is fine at 32"–43" and on a tight budget.
- **4K (UHD)** is worth it at 50" and above, where the extra sharpness is visible. Most streaming and newer content supports it.

## Smart features that matter

- **Built-in streaming** (a proper smart TV with a fast interface) saves you buying a separate box.
- **Enough HDMI ports** (at least 3) for a decoder, a console and a soundbar.
- **Good built-in apps** for the services you actually use.

## Brands and value

TCL, Hisense and Vitron offer strong value; Samsung, LG and Sony cost more but lead on picture quality and software. Whatever you pick, [compare TV prices across stores](/c/tvs) — large items see big price gaps and delivery costs vary.
