# How to Buy a Used or Refurbished Phone Safely in Kenya

Refurbished and "ex-UK" phones are one of the best value plays in the Kenyan market. But the difference between a bargain and a regret comes down to a few checks you must do *before* money changes hands.

## Run through this checklist

- **Battery health.** On iPhone, check Settings → Battery → Battery Health (aim for 85%+). On Android, ask how old the unit is and watch how fast it drains.
- **Is it locked?** Make sure the phone is not iCloud-locked (iPhone) or tied to a Google account (Android). Ask the seller to fully sign out and factory reset in front of you.
- **Check the IMEI.** Dial `*#06#` to get the IMEI, then confirm it is not blacklisted or reported stolen. A clean IMEI also confirms the model and storage.
- **Inspect the screen and body.** Look for dead pixels, screen burn-in, and signs the phone has been opened or water-damaged.
- **Test everything.** Cameras, both SIM slots, fingerprint/face unlock, charging port, speakers, microphone, and Wi-Fi/Bluetooth.

## Where to buy

Reputable refurbished sellers offer a short warranty (even 7–30 days) and a return window. That warranty is worth paying a little extra for — it is your safety net.

## Price sanity-check

Before you agree a price, [compare what the same model costs new and refurbished across stores](/c/smartphones). If a "deal" is barely cheaper than a new unit with full warranty, it is not a deal.

> A used phone with verified battery health, a clean IMEI, and a short warranty is a smart buy. One you couldn't test is a gamble.
