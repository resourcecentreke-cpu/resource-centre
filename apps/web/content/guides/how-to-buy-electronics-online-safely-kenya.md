# How to Buy Electronics Online Safely in Kenya

Buying online opens up better prices and wider choice — but it also attracts scammers. These habits keep you safe.

## Before you pay

- **Compare the price across several stores.** A price far below everyone else is the classic bait. [Comparing first](/c/smartphones) tells you the realistic market price.
- **Check the seller's reputation.** Look for a physical address, a working phone number, reviews, and a clear return policy.
- **Confirm warranty and returns in writing.** Know exactly what is covered and for how long before money moves.

## Paying safely

- **Prefer pay-on-delivery** where available, especially for a first purchase from a new seller.
- **Use traceable payments.** Paying a registered business is safer than sending M-Pesa to a personal number with no recourse.
- **Be wary of "deposit now" pressure.** Urgency ("last unit, pay now to hold it") is a common manipulation tactic.

## On delivery

- **Inspect before you accept.** Power the device on, check it is the right model, storage and colour, and that the box is sealed/genuine.
- **Verify the IMEI** on a phone (`*#06#`) and keep your receipt — you need it for warranty claims.

## Red flags to walk away from

A deal far below market, a seller who refuses pay-on-delivery without explanation, no physical address, and pressure to pay immediately. Trust those instincts.

> The safest online buy is from a reputable store, at a price you have compared, with warranty and returns confirmed in writing.
