# How to Spot a Fake or Counterfeit Phone in Kenya

Counterfeit phones — sometimes sold as "master copies" — can look almost identical to the real thing but use weak chips, fake storage and poor batteries. Here is how to protect yourself.

## Quick checks anyone can do

- **Verify the IMEI.** Dial `*#06#`, note the IMEI, and check it against the manufacturer's records. A genuine phone's IMEI matches its model and is not duplicated.
- **Check the software.** Genuine Android phones run clean, branded software. Fakes often have a slightly "off" interface, missing the real Google Play Store or showing odd default apps.
- **Confirm real storage and RAM.** Fakes lie about specs on the box. A free benchmarking or storage-test app will reveal the true numbers.
- **Inspect build quality.** Misaligned buttons, a too-light body, blurry printing on the back, or a sticker that peels off easily are red flags.
- **Test the cameras.** Counterfeits almost always have poor cameras — slow to focus, grainy, and far below what the spec sheet claims.

## Buy from the right places

The simplest protection is buying from a reputable seller with a physical presence, a warranty, and a return policy. If a price looks far below everyone else for a flagship, that is the warning sign — [compare the realistic price across stores](/c/smartphones) first.

> A genuine phone has a clean IMEI, official software, real storage, and a working warranty. If any of those is missing, walk away.
