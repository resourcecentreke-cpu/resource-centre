It's the question every phone buyer eventually asks. Both make excellent phones, so the right answer depends on what you value. Here's a clear, Kenya-specific breakdown.

## Choose iPhone if you value…

- **Long software support.** iPhones get updates for many years, which protects security and resale value.
- **Resale value.** iPhones hold their price better on the Kenyan second-hand market than almost anything else.
- **Simplicity and the Apple ecosystem.** If you use a Mac, iPad or AirPods, everything just works together.

## Choose Samsung Galaxy if you value…

- **Choice and value.** Samsung covers every price point — from the budget **A-series** to the **S-series** flagships — so there's a Galaxy for every budget.
- **Flexibility.** Expandable features, more customisation, and Android's openness.
- **Big-screen and foldable options** like the Galaxy Z Fold and Z Flip.

## Cameras

Both are top-tier. iPhones tend to give consistent, natural video and reliable point-and-shoot photos. Samsung flagships push higher resolution and more versatile zoom. For most people the difference comes down to preference, not quality.

## Repairs & availability in Kenya

Both brands are widely sold and serviced in Kenya. Genuine parts and authorised repair are available for both; always buy from a seller offering a real warranty.

## Price & how to decide

- On a tight budget, a **Samsung A-series** phone gives you the brand and updates affordably — see the **[midrange](/phones/midrange)** and **[lower-midrange](/phones/lower-midrange)** pages.
- If resale value matters most, an iPhone is the safer long-term bet — compare the latest models on the **[flagship phones](/phones/flagship)** page.
- Whatever you choose, **compare the live price across stores** and check the price history before buying.

> Bottom line: pick iPhone for longevity and resale, Samsung for choice and value. Both will serve you well for years.
