# New vs Refurbished Laptop in Kenya

In Kenya, a refurbished "ex-UK" business laptop and a brand-new budget laptop often cost the same — but they are very different machines. Here is how to choose.

## When refurbished wins

Refurbished business-class laptops (Lenovo ThinkPad, HP EliteBook, Dell Latitude) were built to survive years of corporate use. For the price of a new entry-level consumer laptop you often get a better keyboard, sturdier build, more RAM and a faster processor. If you want maximum performance-per-shilling, refurbished is usually the answer.

## When new wins

- You want a **full manufacturer warranty** and zero history.
- You need the **latest battery life and efficiency** (newer chips run cooler and last longer on a charge).
- You want a specific new feature — a high-resolution screen, the newest ports, or a particular GPU.

## What to check on a refurbished unit

- **Battery health and charge cycles.** A tired battery is the most common weak point; ask for the numbers.
- **Grade.** "Grade A" means minimal cosmetic wear; lower grades cost less but show scratches or dents.
- **Warranty.** Even 3–12 months from the seller makes a big difference.

## The bottom line

If you value performance and build quality and you are comfortable checking battery health, refurbished is often the smarter buy. If you value peace of mind and the latest efficiency, buy new.
