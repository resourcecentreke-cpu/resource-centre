# Which iPhone Should You Buy in Kenya?

iPhones hold their value and stay updated for years, which makes even older models worth considering. The right one depends on your budget and how long you plan to keep it.

## If you want the best, and budget is open

The latest **Pro Max** is the photographer's and power-user's choice — the best cameras, the brightest screen, and the longest battery. The standard **Pro** offers nearly all of it in a smaller body.

## The smart-value pick

Last year's model, bought new or as a clean ex-UK unit, usually costs noticeably less while still getting years of iOS updates. For most people this is the sensible buy.

## The budget iPhone

The standard (non-Pro) current iPhone, or an iPhone two generations old, gives you the iOS experience, great resale value, and reliable performance for everyday use at a much friendlier price.

## Buying tips specific to Kenya

- **New vs ex-UK/ex-US:** refurbished iPhones are hugely popular here and can be excellent value — but [check the battery health and that it is not iCloud-locked](/guides/how-to-buy-a-used-phone-safely-in-kenya) before paying.
- **Watch the storage.** 128GB is the practical minimum; photos and videos fill 64GB fast.
- **Confirm the region.** Some imports have physical SIM only or dual-SIM depending on where they were sold.

Compare [iPhone prices across Kenyan stores](/c/smartphones) before buying — the gap between sellers can be KSh 10,000 or more on the same model.
