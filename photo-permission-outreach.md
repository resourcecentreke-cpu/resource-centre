# Product photo permission — sources & outreach kit

Goal: get written permission (a licence) to use product images, so you can show real
photos legally — with credit where asked. Credit alone isn't enough; the **"yes"** in
a reply email is what makes it legal. Keep every reply you get — that's your proof.

---

## B. Brands / manufacturers (best long-term source)

Brands own the original images and license them to resellers. Two routes:

1. **Icecat authorization (free, scales to all products):** in My Icecat → "Authorized
   Reseller of" → request each brand you stock. Approval = licence, then their photos
   flow into your site automatically.
2. **Direct press/media request** (use the email below):

## C. Free / Creative-Commons sources (credit IS the licence here)

For these, attribution genuinely satisfies copyright — use freely with the required credit:

- **Wikimedia Commons** — commons.wikimedia.org (search the model; check each file's CC licence)
- **Openverse** — openverse.org (filter to commercial-use + modification allowed)
- **Manufacturer press kits** explicitly marked "free to use for press/retail with credit"

Coverage for specific 2026 models is patchy, but worth a look for popular devices.

## E. When permission comes in

1. Save the reply (your proof of licence) — a folder or label in your email is fine.
2. Download the images they allow (or the feed link they give).
3. Drop the files in your **Resourcecentre** folder and tell me **"photos are in"** — I'll
   rename them to the right `<slug>.jpg`, add the credit line on the product page if required,
   and place them. They go live on your next sync.

Start with 3–4 of your most responsive retailers (WhatsApp is fastest) and the Icecat brand
authorizations in parallel — that combination covers the most products the quickest.
